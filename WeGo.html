Software Engineering:
WeGo

January - May 2023

We Go is a multi-cloud project designed as a Transportation as a Service (TaaS) Platform, focusing on autonomous vehicle delivery of medical supplies, particularly vaccines. The project strategically aims to capitalize on high-performance teamwork, Software Engineering (SWE) Best Practices, and emerging technologies to identify and scale innovative online transportation services.

Overview

Development Approach:

  • Agile Methodology: Agile Roadmap drives discovery and delivers incremental stakeholder value. Self-managed scrum teams decompose complexity and demonstrate stakeholder value at the end of sprints. Throughout the sprint, each team member changes their roles to ensure everyone has a chance to become the Scrum Master and take on other tasks.
  • Quality & Improvement: Checkpoints, metrics, and feedback drive continuous improvement. The focus is on strategic, maintainable software over tactical programming, implementing Innovation Best Practices, System Design Best Practices, and Software Lifecycle Best Practices + Tools.

    • Role & Responsibilities

    Quality Assurance (QA), Scrum Master, Dev Ops

  • When iterating through roles, it was essential to research and review previous documentation created by other teammates to continue seamlessly from where the last sprint left off.
  • Keeping up with documentation during each sprint to provide clear guidance for the next team member taking on the role

  • As Scrum Master, I coordinated team meetings, updated the team on stakeholder feedback, and kept the team on track in order to successfully progress to the next sprint

    • Challenges & Solutions

    A challange I have faced and how I overcame it...

      Challenge
    • During my time in software engineering, I faced the challenge of impostor syndrome, doubting my capabilities compared to others. For instance, when I transitioned to the role of DevOps, the team was well into the project. I was in charge of taking on the responsibility of setting up the final server for the vehicle simulator, and the thought of this was intimidating.
      • Solution
    • Despite having no prior knowledge in DevOps and with the previous DevOps engineer unavailable, I heavily relied on existing documentation and research. Driven by determination, I navigated through multiple rounds of error handling, addressed file path issues, and mastered various terminal commands. As a result, I was able to successfully established our third and final server and seamlessly transitioned to my next task before the sprint's end.
